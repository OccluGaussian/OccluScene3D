Introduction

We present a comprehensive benchmark dedicated to image-based 3D reconstruction, which serves as a crucial tool for researchers and developers in this field. The benchmark sequences are meticulously acquired in truly realistic conditions, mirroring the complexity and variability of real-world scenarios. These sequences are captured using a common mobile phone, specifically leveraging its wide-angle mode and landscape orientation. The recordings are made at a frame rate of 60 Hz, which provides a smooth and detailed visual stream, essential for accurate 3D reconstruction. We uniformly extract 3% of the frames from each video sequence, subsequently, we employ COLMAP to estimate the camera intrinsic and extrinsic parameters, which are fundamental for establishing the spatial relationships and geometric properties within the captured scenes. These accurately estimated parameters form the cornerstone for subsequent 3D reconstruction tasks, enabling the development and evaluation of various algorithms with a high degree of precision and reliability.
